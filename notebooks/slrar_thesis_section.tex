\section{Motivation}
% =====================================================================

Attention rollout \cite{abnar2020} aggregates the per-layer attention of a
Vision Transformer (ViT) into a single token-to-token relevance map by the
recursive product $\R=\A^{(1)}\cdots\A^{(L)}$. In deep ViTs this product
suffers \emph{spectral collapse}: because every $\A^{(\ell)}$ is row-stochastic
with $\lambda_1=1$ and $|\lambda_2|\!\approx\!1$, the accumulated map converges
towards a rank-one, near-uniform matrix, erasing the spatial structure that
makes the explanation useful. The \emph{Spectral--Laplacian Regularized
Attention Rollout} (SLR-AR) framework proposes to cure this by inserting, at
every block, (i)~a \emph{spectral-norm bounding} (SNB) step that rescales the
propagation matrix so its largest singular value is at most $\tau<1$, and
(ii)~a \emph{graph-theoretic smoothing} (GTS) step that applies a Laplacian
diffusion kernel $\exp(-\beta\Lap)$ to the token-affinity graph. The reported
gain is large: on ImageNet ViT-B/16 the Spectral Stability Index (SSI,
defined below) rises from $0.02$ (rollout) to $0.24$ (SLR-AR).

This section reports a controlled reimplementation and evaluation of SLR-AR on
our Phase-1 target: a ViT-B/16 fine-tuned on the NCT-CRC-HE-100K /
CRC-VAL-HE-7K colorectal-histology benchmark (nine tissue classes). We find
that, once a numerical instability in the diffusion kernel is corrected, SLR-AR
does \emph{not} reduce spectral collapse on this model; it makes it
monotonically worse across every hyperparameter setting we tried. We trace this
to two structural properties of the method that are independent of the dataset,
and confirm both empirically and analytically.

% =====================================================================
\section{Experimental setup}
% =====================================================================

We evaluate on the external CRC-VAL-HE-7K test split, sampling a
class-balanced subset. The saliency target for every metric is the
model's own predicted class (faithfulness is measured with respect to the
model, not the label). We report the four ablation variants of the framework:
\texttt{rollout} (baseline), \texttt{snb} (SNB only), \texttt{gts} (GTS only)
and \texttt{slrar} (both). The propagation matrix, power iteration, spectral
projection, normalized Laplacian, diffusion kernel and constrained aggregation
are implemented exactly as specified (see the accompanying mathematical
specification), with the resolution $\Q^{(\ell)}=\exp(-\beta\Lap^{(\ell)})\,\Pt^{(\ell)}$
for the otherwise under-determined final propagation matrix. Hyperparameters
follow the paper: $\tau=0.95$, $K=5$ power-iteration steps, $M=8$ Taylor terms,
residual mixing $\alpha=0.5$, and adaptive diffusion scale
$\beta=1/\lambda_2^{(\ell)}$ clamped to $[0.1,20]$.

\paragraph{Well-posedness of the Laplacian.} Attention matrices are
row-stochastic but not symmetric, so we symmetrize the token-affinity graph,
$\W^{(\ell)}=\tfrac12\big(\Pt^{(\ell)}+(\Pt^{(\ell)})^{\!\top}\big)$, before
forming $\Lap^{(\ell)}=\I-(\D^{(\ell)})^{-1/2}\W^{(\ell)}(\D^{(\ell)})^{-1/2}$.
Then $\Lap^{(\ell)}$ is symmetric positive semidefinite with real eigenvalues in
$[0,2]$, and the algebraic connectivity $\lambda_2^{(\ell)}$ driving the adaptive
scale is computed with a symmetric eigensolver. This is the only reading under
which the framework's own eigenvalue claims are well-defined; the
non-symmetrized form $\I-\D^{-1/2}\Pt\D^{-1/2}$ would be non-normal, with
possibly complex spectrum and no well-defined ``second smallest'' eigenvalue.
We likewise note that the spectral-projection step, described as singular-value
\emph{truncation} $\sigma_i\mapsto\min(\sigma_i,\tau)$ but implemented via power
iteration as the \emph{scalar normalization} $\Pt=\Pm\cdot\min(1,\tau/\sigma_1)$,
is the latter throughout this work --- a distinction that is decisive in
Proposition~\ref{prop:snb}.

\paragraph{Metrics.} The primary, label-free indicator is the
\emph{Spectral Stability Index}
\begin{equation}
\SSI(\R)=\frac{|\lambda_2(\R)|}{|\lambda_1(\R)|},\qquad
|\lambda_1|\ge|\lambda_2|\ge\cdots,
\label{eq:ssi}
\end{equation}
where a \emph{higher} value denotes a larger spectral gap and hence
\emph{less} collapse. We additionally report Insertion and Deletion AUC over
patch-level maskings with a Gaussian-blur baseline (appropriate for H\&E
patches, where a black/gray reference is out-of-distribution).

% =====================================================================
\section{A numerical instability in the diffusion kernel}
\label{sec:bug}
% =====================================================================

The diffusion kernel is approximated by a truncated Taylor series
(Eq.~9 of the specification),
$\exp(-\beta\Lap)\approx\sum_{m=0}^{M}\frac{(-\beta)^m}{m!}\Lap^{m}$,
with $M=8$. A Taylor expansion of the exponential converges only when its
argument is small; the truncation error grows without bound once
$\beta\,\lambda_{\max}(\Lap)$ exceeds $\mathcal{O}(1)$. The method's own
adaptive rule $\beta=1/\lambda_2$ \emph{guarantees} a large $\beta$ precisely on
collapsed attention, where the algebraic connectivity $\lambda_2$ is small, so
$\beta$ saturates its clamp. Table~\ref{tab:taylor} contrasts the true spectral
norm of $\exp(-\beta\Lap)$ --- which is provably $\le 1$, since $\Lap$ is
positive semidefinite --- with the Taylor estimate on a representative layer.

\begin{table}[h]
\centering
\caption{Spectral norm of the diffusion kernel: exact matrix exponential
versus the order-$8$ Taylor truncation, as $\beta$ increases. The exact value
is bounded by $1$; the truncation diverges catastrophically.}
\label{tab:taylor}
\begin{tabular}{r r r r}
\toprule
$\beta$ & $\beta\,\lambda_{\max}(\Lap)$ & $\|\exp(-\beta\Lap)\|_2$ (exact) & $\|\cdot\|_2$ (Taylor, $M{=}8$) \\
\midrule
10 & 5.3  & 1.00 & $5.7$ \\
15 & 7.9  & 1.00 & $1.9\times 10^{2}$ \\
20 & 10.6 & 1.00 & $2.2\times 10^{3}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

In our first evaluation this manifested directly: the two GTS-bearing variants
produced propagation matrices with mean spectral norm
$\|\Q\|_2\approx 1.8\times 10^{2}$ (versus $\approx 1$ for the correct kernel)
and an SSI numerically indistinguishable from zero. Replacing the truncation
with the exact matrix exponential (\texttt{torch.linalg.matrix\_exp}; cheap at
$N=197$) removes the instability. \textbf{All results below use the exact
kernel.} We flag this because the truncated series is presented in the source
as an implementation detail with no stability caveat, yet it silently destroys
the method whenever the adaptive $\beta$ is engaged --- a reproducibility hazard
for any downstream user.

two components cannot help, independently of the data.

% =====================================================================
\section{Why the two components cannot reduce collapse}
% =====================================================================

\begin{proposition}[SNB is invariant to the SSI]
\label{prop:snb}
The spectral-projection step rescales each propagation matrix by a scalar,
$\Pt^{(\ell)}=c_\ell\,\Pm^{(\ell)}$ with $c_\ell=\min(1,\tau/\sigma_K)\in(0,1]$.
Hence the aggregated rollout satisfies
$\R_{\mathrm{snb}}=\big(\prod_{\ell}c_\ell\big)\,\R_{\mathrm{rollout}}$, its
eigenvalues are uniformly scaled, and
\[
\SSI(\R_{\mathrm{snb}})
=\frac{|(\prod_\ell c_\ell)\,\lambda_2(\R_{\mathrm{rollout}})|}
       {|(\prod_\ell c_\ell)\,\lambda_1(\R_{\mathrm{rollout}})|}
=\SSI(\R_{\mathrm{rollout}}).
\]
Moreover the min--max-normalized \texttt{[CLS]} saliency is unchanged, as a
positive scalar cancels in $(S-\min S)/(\max S-\min S)$.
\end{proposition}

Proposition~\ref{prop:snb} explains the exact tie in Table~\ref{tab:ablation}
(confirmed to six decimals). The SSI, being a \emph{ratio} of eigenvalues, is by
construction blind to any global rescaling. Since the paper's power-iteration
form of SNB \emph{is} a global rescaling, SNB cannot contribute to the reported
SSI gain; a genuine change would require a non-scalar operator such as the
rank-one deflation the paper defines but never applies.

\begin{proposition}[GTS is a consensus operator that closes the gap]
\label{prop:gts}
The normalized Laplacian $\Lap$ is positive semidefinite with a zero eigenvalue
whose eigenvector is the (degree-weighted) constant mode. Its diffusion kernel
$\exp(-\beta\Lap)=\sum_i e^{-\beta\lambda_i}\bm{\phi}_i\bm{\phi}_i^{\top}$ has
top eigenvalue $e^{0}=1$ on that constant ``DC'' mode and damps every other mode
by $e^{-\beta\lambda_i}<1$, \emph{more} strongly for larger $\lambda_i$. It is
therefore a low-pass (averaging) operator with unit spectral radius attained
only on the DC mode. Aggregating such kernels across depth,
$\R=\prod_\ell \exp(-\beta_\ell\Lap^{(\ell)})$, is a consensus iteration: the DC
component is preserved while all others contract geometrically, so
$\SSI(\R)\to 0$ as $L$ grows.
\end{proposition}

The consensus mechanism is quantitatively sharp. For a fixed kernel with
$\beta=1/\lambda_2$, a single application has a perfectly healthy gap
($\SSI=e^{-1}=0.368$), but the depth-wise product collapses geometrically:
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{l r r r r r}
\toprule
depth $\ell$ & 1 & 2 & 4 & 8 & 12 \\
\midrule
$\SSI(\R^{(\ell)})$ & $0.368$ & $0.135$ & $1.8\times10^{-2}$
& $3.4\times10^{-4}$ & $6.1\times10^{-6}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The $\ell=12$ value matches the aggressive-diffusion rows of
Table~\ref{tab:sweep}. This is the crux: \emph{diffusion suppresses
high-frequency modes, and under the eigenvalue-ratio definition of collapse
(Eq.~\ref{eq:ssi}), suppressing every mode except the dominant one \textbf{is}
collapse.} The framework's stated intuition --- ``attenuating high-frequency
eigenmodes prevents collapse'' --- conflates two different objects: it does
smooth the \emph{spatial} frequency content of a single attention map (which can
yield visually cleaner heatmaps), but it accelerates collapse of the
\emph{aggregation spectrum}. The two SLR-AR components thus sit on opposite
sides of the metric they are meant to improve: SNB cannot move it, and GTS moves
it the wrong way.

\paragraph{Row-stochasticity is not the remedy.} A natural objection is that the
symmetric kernel $\exp(-\beta\Lap)$ is not row-stochastic
($\exp(-\beta\Lap)\mathbf{1}\neq\mathbf{1}$, since $\Lap\mathbf{1}\neq\mathbf{0}$
for the symmetrically normalized Laplacian of a non-symmetric $\Pt$), and that
the random-walk Laplacian $\Lap_{\mathrm{rw}}=\I-\D^{-1}\Pt$ --- whose
exponential \emph{is} exactly row-stochastic --- should be used instead. This
does not change the conclusion. A random walk is itself a consensus process, so
its depth-wise aggregation collapses at least as fast: substituting
$\Lap_{\mathrm{rw}}$ yields $\SSI\approx3.7\times10^{-6}$ at $L=12$, versus
$6.1\times10^{-6}$ for the symmetric kernel. Row-stochasticity governs whether
$\Q^{(\ell)}$ is a valid probability propagator, not whether the aggregation
preserves a spectral gap; it is orthogonal to the collapse mechanism of
Proposition~\ref{prop:gts}.

% =====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}
% =====================================================================

On our colorectal-histology ViT-B/16, SLR-AR does not deliver its central
promise. After correcting a Taylor-truncation instability that otherwise
masquerades as a catastrophic failure (Section~\ref{sec:bug}), the method
reduces the Spectral Stability Index below the plain-rollout baseline in every
one of twelve hyperparameter configurations (Section~\ref{sec:results}), and two
data-independent arguments (Propositions~\ref{prop:snb}--\ref{prop:gts}) show
why: spectral-norm bounding is a scalar operation to which the ratio-based SSI is
invariant, and Laplacian diffusion is a consensus operator whose depth-wise
aggregation drives the spectral gap to zero. We conclude that, as specified, the
framework cannot prevent spectral collapse measured this way. A component that
genuinely enlarges the spectral gap must be \emph{non-scalar and gap-enhancing}
--- e.g.\ deflation of the dominant rank-one component, or high-pass rather than
low-pass filtering of the aggregation spectrum --- which we leave to future work.
